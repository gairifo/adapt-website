The problem with staying

ColdFusion developers are retiring and not being replaced. Every year, your bus factor gets worse. Adobe's licensing costs keep climbing while platform investment stagnates. And every new CVE disclosure is a scramble — you can't patch fast enough, and you can't audit what you can't staff.

→

The problem with manual rewrites

ColdFusion codebases are deceptively complex — CFML templates, CFCs, stored procedures, and inline SQL are tightly interwoven. Manual rewrites take 2-4 years and routinely lose business logic. Teams burn out reverse-engineering undocumented behavior.

Inside the ColdFusion factory

AI parses your CFML codebase. Architects own the structure.

01

CFML parsing

Our AI ingests your ColdFusion templates, components (CFCs), custom tags, configuration files, and embedded SQL. It builds a complete map of every page, business rule, data flow, and integration. Every artifact is logged and inspectable.

02

Target generation

The AI emits idiomatic code in your chosen target stack — Node.js, .NET, Java/Spring, Python (Django, FastAPI), or React. SQL and stored procedure logic is preserved and restructured for the target ORM or data layer. Senior architects review every structural decision.

03

Equivalence testing

Before any cutover, the AI synthesizes behavioral tests that compare new system output against the live ColdFusion system. Page by page, query by query. You migrate on a passing diff, not on faith.
