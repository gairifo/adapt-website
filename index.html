The replatforming math has changed

Rewrites used to take years. AI breaks the trade-off.

→

The old way

You hire a system integrator. They quote 24–36 months and 8–15M euros for a full rewrite. Half the team leaves before it ships. The new system inherits old assumptions and never quite reaches feature parity. You stay on the legacy platform paying escalating license fees while the rewrite drifts.

→

The Adapt way

AI parses your source codebase — OML, Forms metadata, legacy AST — and emits idiomatic modern code in your target stack. Senior architects own the structural decisions and review every commit. Phased releases, parallel running, and a working modern system in months, not years. License costs drop. Your team learns the new stack as it ships.

Inside the factory

AI does the work that used to consume the rewrite.

01

Source parsing

Our AI reads your source platform — whether that's a low-code DSL, a legacy framework, a 4GL, or a tangle of bespoke metadata — and builds a complete map of entities, screens, business rules, integrations, and data flows. Every artifact is inspectable. No black box.

02

Target generation

The AI emits idiomatic code in whichever target your team chooses — modern frameworks (Java/Spring, .NET, Node, Python, React, Vue) or modern low-code platforms (OutSystems, Mendix, Power Platform, ServiceNow, Salesforce). Senior architects shape the target architecture and own the non-trivial calls. AI handles the volume; humans handle the judgment.

03

Equivalence testing

Before any cutover, the AI synthesizes behavioral tests that compare new system output against legacy system output. You don't migrate on faith — you migrate on a passing diff.
