The Mendix cost trap

Staying costs millions. A manual rewrite takes years. There's a third option.

→

The problem with staying

Mendix licensing scales with your app count. Every new app, every new user tier pushes the invoice higher. The proprietary runtime means you're deploying on Mendix's terms, not yours. And the niche talent market makes scaling your team slow and expensive.

→

The problem with manual rewrites

Traditional rewrites of Mendix estates take 2-4 years, cost millions, and almost always lose business logic in translation. Microflows, domain models, and page logic are deeply coupled to the platform — extracting them manually is error-prone and exhausting.

Your target, your choice

One source. Multiple proven target paths.

→

Mendix → Modern Framework

Go high-code. Java/Spring, .NET, Node.js, React, Python — for teams that want full ownership, abundant talent, and no platform dependency. The AI extracts and converts microflows, domain models, and page logic into idiomatic application code.

→

Mendix → OutSystems

Stay in low-code but switch platforms. Useful when the team wants rapid delivery but different licensing terms or a better fit for the org's scale.

→

Mendix → Hybrid

Modern back-end with low-code front-end, or vice versa. Common where part of the org wants control and part wants velocity.

Inside the Mendix factory

AI parses your Mendix models and microflows. Architects own the structure.

01

Model & microflow parsing

Our AI ingests your Mendix project — domain models, microflows, pages, nanoflows, integrations, and scheduled events. It builds a complete dependency map. Every artifact is logged and inspectable.

02

Target generation

The AI emits idiomatic code or configuration in your chosen target — Java/Spring, .NET, Node.js, React, Python, OutSystems, or another stack. Senior architects review every structural decision.

03

Equivalence testing

Before any cutover, the AI synthesizes behavioral tests that compare new system output against the live Mendix system. You migrate on a passing diff, not on faith.
