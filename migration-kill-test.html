The Migration Kill Test

An honest stay-or-go read, before you spend a cent on a full audit.

The Migration Kill Test is a bounded, evidence-based feasibility check. You give us three things; we give you a stay/retire/detach/replatform recommendation, the principal risks and unknowns, and — if it's a fit — what a paid audit would need to confirm. It's designed to disqualify honestly, not to manufacture a sales opportunity.

What you give us

Three artifacts. Nothing more.

01

A renewal, support, or strategy document

Whatever captures the forcing event — a license renewal notice, an end-of-support timeline, or an internal modernization brief. This tells us the clock you're working against.

02

A high-level application & integration inventory

What exists and what it talks to — not source code, not credentials. An architecture diagram, a module list, or an honest "here's roughly what we have" is enough to start.

03

Five critical business workflows

The handful of processes that would actually hurt if they broke. These anchor the feasibility read in what matters to the business, not just the codebase.

What you get back

A bounded, honest answer — including "we don't know yet."

→

Stay / retire / detach / replatform

A clear recommendation, or an explicit "insufficient evidence" if that's the honest answer. We're not incentivized to find a replatforming case where one doesn't exist — a Kill Test that disqualifies you is a good outcome, not a failed one.

→

Principal risks, unknowns, and next evidence

What we're confident about, what we're not, and exactly what a paid audit would need to confirm before you commit real budget — plus a provisional view of what a partner-delivered piece looks like versus what Adapt owns, if a channel partner introduced you.

Commercial model

Free, paid, or credited — set case by case.

Depending on account size and how you reached us, the Kill Test is offered free for named strategic accounts, at a fixed low price, or as a paid step fully credited against the audit if you proceed. Ask directly — we'll tell you which applies before you commit anything.
