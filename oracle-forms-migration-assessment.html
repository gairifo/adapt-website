Oracle Forms assessment

Oracle Forms migration assessment: what a real one includes.

"Assessment" gets used loosely in this market — sometimes it means a genuine technical read, sometimes it means a form count and a rough quote dressed up as analysis. Here's what we think a real one actually needs to cover.

Draft. Pending Pedro's review before publication — not yet linked from nav, footer, or the Oracle Forms page — held to the same approval gate as the other new Phase 2/3 pages.

What it needs to cover

Five things, at minimum.

01

Real form and PL/SQL inventory

Not a rough count from a conversation — an actual inventory of forms, reports, and PL/SQL objects, parsed from the source, with complexity scored per module.

02

Target-neutral options

A genuine assessment presents APEX, Java, and .NET honestly — not steered toward whichever target the assessor happens to deliver.

03

Data and integration strategy

How your Oracle data and any integrations with other systems carry over — not deferred to "we'll figure it out during delivery."

04

A real cost model

License and maintenance economics compared against migration cost — specific to your estate, not an industry-average slide.

05

A fixed quote for what comes next

If migration is the right call, a firm quote for the delivery phase — decided with real estate data, not a placeholder pending a "detailed scoping" that never quite happens.
