The Oracle Forms dilemma

The platform won't evolve. A manual rewrite takes years. There's a third option.

→

The problem with staying

Oracle Forms is in sustaining support — no new features, no modern UI, no API-first capabilities. The developers who built your system are retiring, and replacements barely exist. Every year you stay, the risk of an unplanned, emergency migration grows. Meanwhile, Oracle licensing costs keep rising with every audit cycle.

→

The problem with manual rewrites

A typical Oracle Forms estate contains decades of embedded PL/SQL business logic, complex triggers, and tightly coupled Reports. Manual rewrites take 3–5 years, cost millions, and almost always lose business logic in translation. Most stall or fail outright — the institutional knowledge leaves before the new system ships.

Your target, your choice

Oracle Forms → OutSystems

Stay in low-code. Preserve the rapid-delivery model your team knows, but on a modern platform with a web-native UI, API-first integrations, and a growing developer ecosystem. Ideal when the team values speed-to-feature over full code ownership.

Oracle Forms → Oracle APEX

Go high-code. Node.js, .NET, Java/Spring, React, Python — for teams that want full ownership, abundant talent, and no platform dependency. The AI extracts and converts PL/SQL business logic into idiomatic application code in whichever stack your team will own next.

Inside the Oracle Forms factory

AI parses your FMB files and PL/SQL. Architects own the structure.

01

Forms & Reports parsing

Our AI ingests your FMB, FMX, and RDF files — every form, trigger, LOV, record group, PL/SQL program unit, and Reports layout. It builds a complete dependency map of entities, screens, business rules, and data flows. Every artifact is logged and inspectable.

02

Target generation

The AI emits idiomatic code or configuration in your chosen target — APEX pages, OutSystems screens, or high-code in Node.js, .NET, Java/Spring, React, or Python. PL/SQL business logic is extracted, refactored, and placed where it belongs in the target architecture. Senior architects review every structural decision.

03

Equivalence testing

Before any cutover, the AI synthesizes behavioral tests that compare new system output against the live Oracle Forms system. Screen-by-screen, query-by-query. You migrate on a passing diff, not on faith.
