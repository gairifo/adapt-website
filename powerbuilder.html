The problem with staying

Every year on PowerBuilder narrows your options. The developer pool has effectively vanished — there is no training pipeline, no bootcamp, no university teaching PowerScript. Appeon's platform updates are incremental at best. And your DataWindow-centric architecture is invisible to modern DevOps, CI/CD, and cloud tooling.

→

The problem with manual rewrites

PowerBuilder applications are built around DataWindows — a uniquely powerful but deeply proprietary abstraction for data retrieval, presentation, and update. Manual rewrites invariably underestimate the complexity hidden in DataWindow expressions, computed columns, validation rules, and update characteristics. Most projects stall at 60% completion.

Inside the PowerBuilder factory

AI parses your DataWindows and PowerScript. Architects own the structure.

01

PBL parsing

Our AI ingests your PowerBuilder libraries (PBLs) — DataWindows, DataStores, windows, user objects, menus, functions, and PowerScript. It maps every DataWindow column, computed field, validation rule, retrieval argument, and update property. Every artifact is logged and inspectable.

02

Target generation

The AI emits idiomatic code in your chosen target stack — .NET (with data grids or Blazor), Java/Spring, React + API, Node.js, or Python. DataWindow logic is converted into equivalent data access layers, grid components, and business rules. Senior architects review every structural decision.

03

Equivalence testing

Before any cutover, the AI synthesizes behavioral tests comparing new system output against the live PowerBuilder application. DataWindow by DataWindow, query by query. You migrate on a passing diff.
